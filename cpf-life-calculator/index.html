Important — please read. This tool produces illustrative estimates only and is not financial advice, an offer, or a guarantee of any payout. Figures start from 2026 CPF parameters (age-based contribution & allocation rates, $8,000 monthly wage ceiling, 2.5% OA / 4% Special-MediSave-Retirement interest, MediSave/Basic Healthcare Sum $79,000, BRS $110,200 · FRS $220,400 · ERS $440,800). Interest follows CPF's rules: extra interest of +1% on the first $60,000 of combined balances below 55 (and +2%/+1% on the first $30k/next $30k from 55), capped at $20,000 of OA and applied in the order RA→OA→SA→MA; because CPF computes interest monthly and contributions only earn interest from the following month, this year's contributions are credited about half a year of interest (an annual approximation of CPF's monthly method). Because these limits rise over time, the projection grows them forward on historical trends — BHS ~4.5%/yr (frozen at age 65), the wage ceiling ~3%/yr, and the retirement sums ~3.5%/yr — and assumes contributions continue to age 55, after which balances grow at CPF interest with no further contributions or withdrawals. Payouts are shown in future (nominal) dollars; the "today's dollars" figures discount them to 2026 at an assumed 2.5% long-run inflation rate (Singapore's inflation has been broadly stable). The RSTU top-up you enter is treated as a cash top-up to your SA, capped each year at the prevailing Full Retirement Sum (the first $8,000/yr also qualifies for income-tax relief). The VC-3A contribution is a voluntary contribution split across OA/SA/MA by your age's allocation rates and capped at the CPF Annual Limit ($37,740, grown on trend) minus mandatory CPF; it is the route that also builds your Ordinary Account, and its OA/SA portion does not attract tax relief. For self-employed persons, only MediSave is compulsory (modelled at 8–10.5% of net trade income by age, on income above $6,000 up to the annual ceiling); OA/SA build only from MediSave that overflows the BHS and from voluntary top-ups, so payouts are typically far lower unless you top up. CPF LIFE payouts are modelled from CPF Board's published Standard-Plan estimates (male basis, ~7% female adjustment) and interpolated; Basic and Escalating figures are approximations. These growth and inflation rates are assumptions, not guarantees — actual CPF rules, interest rates, limits, and payouts are set by the CPF Board and change over time. Verify any figure with the official CPF LIFE Estimator and a licensed adviser before acting.
